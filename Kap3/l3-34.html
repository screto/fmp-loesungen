Aufgabe 3.34 - Wurzeln vereinfachen

Aufgabe 3.35 »
Aufgabe: Vereinfachen Sie:
a) $\sqrt[3]{64} \cdot \sqrt[4]{625}$
$\sqrt[3]{64} = \sqrt[3]{4^3} = 4$
$\sqrt[4]{625} = \sqrt[4]{5^4} = 5$

Resultat: $\sqrt[3]{64} \cdot \sqrt[4]{625} = 4 \cdot 5 = 20$
b) $\sqrt[3]{16} \cdot \sqrt[4]{64} \cdot \sqrt[6]{2}$
$\sqrt[3]{16} = 16^{\frac{1}{3}} = (2^4)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 2^{\frac{4}{3}}$
$\sqrt[4]{64} = 64^{\frac{1}{4}} = (2^6)^{\frac{1}{4}} = 2^{\frac{6}{4}} = 2^{\frac{3}{2}}$
$\sqrt[6]{2} = 2^{\frac{1}{6}}$

Gesamtexponent: $\frac{4}{3} + \frac{3}{2} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{8}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{18}{6} = 3$

Resultat: $2^3 = 8$
c) $\frac{\sqrt[3]{5} \cdot \sqrt[7]{6}}{\sqrt[8]{4}}$
$\sqrt[3]{5} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}}$
$\sqrt[7]{6} = 6^{\frac{1}{7}}$
$\sqrt[8]{4} = 4^{\frac{1}{8}} = (2^2)^{\frac{1}{8}} = 2^{\frac{1}{4}}$

Resultat: $\frac{5^{\frac{1}{3}} \cdot 6^{\frac{1}{7}}}{2^{\frac{1}{4}}}$

(Kann nicht weiter vereinfacht werden)
d) $\frac{\sqrt[4]{7} \cdot \sqrt[5]{11}}{\sqrt[8]{17} \cdot \sqrt[3]{10}}$
$\sqrt[4]{7} = 7^{\frac{1}{4}}$
$\sqrt[5]{11} = 11^{\frac{1}{5}}$
$\sqrt[8]{17} = 17^{\frac{1}{8}}$
$\sqrt[3]{10} = 10^{\frac{1}{3}}$

Resultat: $\frac{7^{\frac{1}{4}} \cdot 11^{\frac{1}{5}}}{17^{\frac{1}{8}} \cdot 10^{\frac{1}{3}}}$

(Kann nicht weiter vereinfacht werden)
e) $\frac{\sqrt[4]{2\,401} \cdot \sqrt[5]{32\,768}}{\sqrt[7]{823\,543} \cdot \sqrt[9]{512}}$
$2\,401 = 7^4$, also $\sqrt[4]{2\,401} = \sqrt[4]{7^4} = 7$
$32\,768 = 2^{15}$, also $\sqrt[5]{32\,768} = \sqrt[5]{2^{15}} = 2^3 = 8$
$823\,543 = 7^7$, also $\sqrt[7]{823\,543} = \sqrt[7]{7^7} = 7$
$512 = 2^9$, also $\sqrt[9]{512} = \sqrt[9]{2^9} = 2$

Resultat: $\frac{7 \cdot 8}{7 \cdot 2} = \frac{56}{14} = 4$
